Helen Keller

The life and work of the first blind and deaf advocate

Helen Keller in profile, 1904. Photo from Wikipedia

Here's a timeline of Helen Keller's life:


  • 1880 - Born in Tuscumbia, Alabama.

  • 1882 - Helen contracts an illness and loses her sight and hearing. Helen's mother, determined to help her daughter, makes contact with the Perkins Institute for the Blind.

  • 1887 - Anne Sullivan arrives in Alabama to begin teaching Helen. Helen later referred to this day as “my soul’s birthday”.

  • 1903 - The Story of My Life, Keller’s autobiography and first book is published

  • 1904 - Helen graduates from Radcliffe College. She is the first deaf blind person to earn a bachelor's degree.

  • 1904 - Helen buys a home in Wrentham, Massachusetts

  • 1905 - Helen gets married to John Macy

  • 1918 - Braille is established as the single writing system for blind person in the USA. Helen plays a large role in advocating for this.

  • 1919 - Stars in silent film, Deliverance

  • 1924 - Begins life-long work as the public spokesperson for the American Foundation for the Blind.

  • 1946 - Helen begins advocacy work on behalf of people with disabilities and inspires many governments to establish schools for the blind and deaf. She traveled to 35 countries in 11 years.

  • 1955 - Wins an Oscar for Helen Keller in Her Story, a documentary about her life, directed by Nancy Hamilton.

  • 1936: Anne Sullivan Macy, Keller's beloved Teacher and companion, dies on October 20.

  • 1968 - Helen dies peacefully at home in Connecticut

"The only thing worse than being blind is having sight but no vision" - Helen Keller
